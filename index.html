Research Statement

Measurement,
Fairness & Language

My research investigates the validity and fairness of AI-assisted scoring in educational assessment, with particular attention to how automated and human raters differ in their evaluation behavior. I apply Many-Facet Rasch Measurement (MFRM) to disentangle rater effects from construct-relevant variation, and draw on Natural Language Processing to examine scoring quality in language assessment contexts. A unifying concern across this work is ensuring that measurement is equitable—and that inferences drawn from test scores remain defensible when rater characteristics, human or machine, introduce systematic bias.
